Ayurveda, an ancient system of medicine originating from the Indian subcontinent, has garnered increasing attention in contemporary healthcare practices worldwide . Key principles such as the concept of doshas (Vata, Pitta, Kapha), the importance of balance, and the role of diet, herbs, yoga, and meditation are discussed. conclusion, while Ayurveda offers promising avenues for enhancing healthcare outcomes, further rigorous research, interdisciplinary collaboration, and regulatory measures are essential to realize its full potential in modern healthcare settings. Embracing the synergies between Ayurveda and contemporary medicine holds the promise of delivering personalized, holistic care to individuals worldwide, fostering health and well-being across diverse populations. Moreover, the review discusses the integration of Ayurveda into mainstream healthcare systems, emphasizing the need for collaborative efforts between Ayurvedic practitioners and conventional medical professionals. Challenges such as limited research funding, cultural biases, and the need for evidence-based practice guidelines are acknowledged, along with potential strategies to address these barriers.
